Hi!

I recently graduated with an M.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering and an M.Sc. in Mechatronics & Robotics from Leibniz University Hannover, Germany. During my studies, I conducted research at the Institute of Mechatronic Systems at the Leibniz University Hannover under the supervision of Thomas Seel and the Continuum Robotics Lab at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Jessica Burgner-Kahrs.

Prior to this, I completed a dual study program with a B.Eng. Mechanical Engineering at the University of Applied Sciences Münster, Germany, in partnership with Schmitz Cargobull AG, following the distinguished German dual education pathway, which combines vocational training and academic studies. During this program, I qualified as a Mechatronics Technician, rotated through several R&D departments, and later joined full-time as a System Development Engineer for vehicle electrical architectures.

My research interests focus on soft and continuum robotics, model- and learning-based control (including MPC), kinematic and dynamic modelling, and machine learning.

Moving forward, I aim to widen my skill sets and expand my work in robot perception and semantic understanding, mobile robotics and manipulation, and state estimation.
